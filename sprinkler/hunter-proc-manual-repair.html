Sprinkler Head Repair & Maintenance — Hunter (Complete Guide)

This section walks you through common problems with Hunter brand sprinkler heads (rotors, sprays, rotors like PGP/PGP-ADJ, Pro-Spray, PROS-04, MP Rotator family), how to diagnose them, step‑by‑step repair, replacement parts, and regular maintenance to keep your system running well.

Who this is for

  • Homeowners who want to fix noisy, leaking, or uneven sprinklers.
  • DIY landscapers maintaining irrigation systems.
  • Technicians who need a quick checklist for Hunter heads.

Tools & parts you’ll likely need

Tools
  • Flat-head and Phillips screwdrivers
  • Needle-nose pliers
  • Adjustable wrench
  • Razor knife or utility knife
  • Small brush (toothbrush)
  • Replacement nozzles (Hunter nozzles / MP Rotator nozzles)
  • Replacement risers or popup bodies (if broken)
  • Silicone grease or plumbers’ grease (small amount)
  • Towels and a small bucket (to catch water)
Common replacement parts
  • Hunter nozzle kits (by series)
  • MP Rotator replacement nozzles and seals
  • Seals and O‑rings for pop‑ups
  • Risers (6" or 12") and valve box parts
  • Filter screens
  • Inlet or body for PGP/PGJ rotors

Before you start — quick safety checklist

  • Turn off the controller or disable the program that controls the zone you'll work on (see earlier sections on disabling Program B).
  • Shut off the water supply to the irrigation zone if possible (manifold or isolation valve).
  • Wear gloves and eyewear if you’ll be digging or dealing with debris.
  • Keep the area free of pets and children while you work.

Common problems and fixes

1) Low or uneven water coverage

Symptoms: Wet and dry patches, poor spray distance.

  1. Inspect the nozzle: remove debris and check for clogs. For spray heads, unscrew nozzle and rinse. For MP Rotators, pull out the rotor and flush the screen.
  2. Check the filter screen inside the riser — clean or replace.
  3. Verify water pressure: low pressure reduces rotor throw. If multiple heads low, check for closed valve or clogged line.
  4. Replace worn nozzles with correct Hunter nozzle (match radius and pattern).

2) Head won’t pop up / stuck down

Symptoms: Head stays retracted or only partially pops up.

  1. Dig around the head and remove debris, dirt or roots that might block motion.
  2. With the zone off, manually pull up the pop‑up to see if it moves freely—inspect the riser for cracks.
  3. Inspect and clean the inner riser and outer body. Use a toothbrush to remove grit.
  4. Check O‑ring/seal condition; replace if flattened or damaged.
  5. If shaft is bent or cracked, replace the entire pop‑up or riser assembly.

3) Head leaks or drips after station shuts off

Symptoms: Water seeps from head after zone stops.

  1. Check the solenoid/valve at the valve box — sometimes valves do not fully close and leak into the lateral line.
  2. If valve seals are fine, remove the head and inspect the seat and nozzle for damage.
  3. Replace worn seals or nozzles. Use PTFE tape sparingly on threaded risers if needed.
  4. After repair, run a short test cycle and observe for persistent leakage.

4) Rotor sprays with uneven arc or spray pattern

Symptoms: Gaps, overspray, or wrong pattern.

  1. Adjust the arc or radius using manufacturer instructions (many Hunter rotors have a small flat screwdriver slot on top to set arc).
  2. Clean the rotor drive and nozzle ports — debris can change the pattern.
  3. Replace nozzle with correct size; verify the nozzle seat is intact.

5) Nozzle pops out or shoots off

Symptoms: High flow pushing nozzle out or missing nozzle.

  1. Ensure the nozzle clip or retaining tab (if present) is in place.
  2. Replace damaged nozzle or retaining parts.
  3. If excessive pressure is the cause, install a pressure regulator or use a different nozzle suited for higher pressure (MP Rotators are pressure‑tolerant).

Step‑by‑step: Replace a spray head nozzle (common task)

  1. Turn off the zone water (controller and/or isolation valve).
  2. Unscrew the nozzle assembly by hand (counter‑clockwise) from the pop‑up riser.
  3. Inspect the filter screen below the nozzle — pull out and clean.
  4. Select the replacement nozzle (match spray radius and pattern). Hunter nozzles are labeled; MP Rotator nozzles by flow rate.
  5. Screw new nozzle in by hand until snug. Do not overtighten plastic threads; finish finger‑tight plus 1/4 turn if needed.
  6. Turn the water back on and run a short station test; observe pattern and adjust if needed.

Replacing a pop‑up riser or full body

  1. Turn off water to the zone and relieve pressure by running the zone briefly.
  2. Dig around the head so you can access the body and coupling.
  3. Unscrew the head from the lateral pipe (or remove coupling). If glued PVC, cut the lateral and install a replacement riser with a coupling or repair kit.
  4. Install new riser/body and ensure it is plumb (vertical) and at proper height (top of head flush with lawn grade).
  5. Backfill carefully — avoid packing directly against the head to allow free movement.

Routine maintenance schedule

IntervalTask
MonthlyVisual check for clogged nozzles, broken heads, signs of overspray.
QuarterlyClean filter screens, check for leaks at valves, verify coverage.
Annually (spring)Full system check: replace worn nozzles, adjust arcs, test pressure, winterize if necessary later in year.
Before winterBlow out lines or fully drain system to prevent freeze damage (if applicable).

Winterization & freeze protection

  1. Isolate and drain lines where possible, or use compressed air to blow out the lateral lines (follow proper PSI guidelines — do not exceed valve or pipe ratings).
  2. Remove and store adjustable nozzles if recommended by manufacturer.
  3. Ensure controller and valves are protected from freezing temperatures.

Troubleshooting quick cheat

  • No water at head: Check upstream valve, wiring, and controller program for that zone.
  • Head is noisy: Clean debris and check for damaged drive gears (for rotors).
  • Always running / won't shut off: Check the zone valve solenoid and actuator — may need valve rebuild.

When to call a professional

  • Multiple broken laterals or suspected mainline damage.
  • Valve body submerged or heavily corroded.
  • Complex controller or sensor wiring issues beyond simple settings.
Pro tip: Keep a small maintenance kit with common nozzles, O‑rings, and a spare riser in a weatherproof box near the controller or garage — saves time on common repairs.

Reference Cheat‑Sheets

Clear all Program B start times — quick cheat
  1. Dial → Start Times
  2. PRG → B
  3. Set Start 1–4 → OFF
  4. Dial → Run
Disable Program B watering days
  1. Dial → Water Days
  2. PRG → B
  3. Turn off every day
  4. Dial → Run
Reset Program B
  1. On Program B: press-and-hold PRG (model dependent)
  2. Verify values are cleared
