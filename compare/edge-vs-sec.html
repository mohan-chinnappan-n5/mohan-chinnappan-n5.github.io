Edge and Express Connect

A comparison between Salesforce Edge and Salesforce Express Connect (SEC)

Salesforce Edge

Salesforce Edge provides faster access to Salesforce by reducing network latency, enhancing performance for users distributed across multiple regions.

Pros

  • Improved application performance. Customers get benefits such as faster download times no matter where their org is hosted in Salesforceâ€™s data centers.
  • Directs customer requests to the closest Salesforce location where Salesforce Edge Network is deployed. At that location, Salesforce Edge Network provides a range of services, including:
    • TLS Termination - Brings TLS termination closer to the users.
    • Static Content Caching - The static content is cached at closer to the user.
    • Route Optimization - Edge will find the fastest route for you at that moment in time.
    • Network TCP Optimization - helps to data move more quickly and efficiently across the network.

Cons

  • Public Internet will be used between the user and the Edge Network

Salesforce Express Connect

Salesforce Express Connect provides a direct, private connection between Salesforce data centers and your organization's network, bypassing the public internet for more secure and reliable communication.

Pros

  • Increased security with private network connections.
  • More consistent and predictable performance.
  • Lower latency due to direct connections.
  • Bypasses public internet, reducing risks of internet congestion.
  • Helps to meet industry regulatory and compliance demands.

Cons

  • Requires setup and configuration of private lines.
  • Higher costs for dedicated network infrastructure.
  • Does not provide direct network connectivity to Hyperforce or non-Hyperforce Public Cloud environments
