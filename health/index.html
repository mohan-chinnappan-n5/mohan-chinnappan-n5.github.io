Good and Bad Food Habits

Understanding what's good for your body and what to avoid

Good Food Habits

Sauerkraut High in vitamin C, probiotics, and lactic acid. Easy to digest.
Arugula Rich in phytonutrients, low in oxalates, anti-inflammatory.
Wild-Caught Salmon High in omega-3, selenium, and vitamin K2. Anti-inflammatory.
Cod Liver Rich in vitamins A, D, K2, and omega-3 fatty acids.
Grass-Fed Hamburger Good source of amino acids, iron, and low on the insulin index.
Pecans High in antioxidants, zinc, and easy to digest.
Extra Virgin Olive Oil Rich in vitamin E, antioxidants, anti-aging, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Bad Food Habits

Granola Bars Contain GMOs, high sugar, and unhealthy ingredients.
Agave Syrup High in fructose, processed by the liver like a toxin.
Flavored Yogurt High in sugar, probiotics often don't survive digestion.
Non-Dairy Creamers Contain unhealthy oils, high fructose corn syrup, and trans fats.
Soy Milk Can cause estrogenic effects, not real milk.
Fast-Food Lettuce Covered with preservatives, unhealthy dressing with trans fats.
Puffed Cereals Highly processed, linked to negative health effects in animal studies.

Water Intake

Water is essential for good health. On average, men should drink about 3.7 liters (125 ounces) of water per day, while women should aim for about 2.7 liters (91 ounces). Factors like activity level, climate, and health conditions may affect individual water needs.

Daily Exercise

Incorporating regular exercise into your daily routine is crucial for overall health. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise, such as walking, swimming, or cycling, five days a week. Physical activity helps improve cardiovascular health, boost mood, enhance sleep, and maintain a healthy weight. Managing Hypertension

Managing hypertension is crucial for maintaining heart health and reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases. Based on Dr. Berg's recommendations, focus on the following strategies:

  • Reduce sodium intake and avoid processed foods that are high in salt.
  • Increase potassium-rich foods, such as leafy greens and bananas, to help balance sodium levels.
  • Incorporate magnesium-rich foods like nuts, seeds, and whole grains into your diet.
  • Maintain a healthy weight through balanced nutrition and regular exercise.
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques and adequate sleep.
